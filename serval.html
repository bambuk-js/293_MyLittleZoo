The Serval

About the Serval

The serval is a medium-sized wild cat which lives in sub-Saharan Africa. It is the only species in its genus.

The look

The serval has the longest legs of all cats, relative to its size. Not only can it run fast, but it is an amazing jumper: two to three metres from a standing start. Its length as an adult is about 80cm (body) and 40cm (tail). There are a number of subspecies, and its geographical range is from the Cape of South Africa to Algeria, wherever the circumstances are right for it. It is lightly built, with very large ears and long legs. The back of the ears is black with a big white spot, and the tip of the tail is black. These signs serve as signals to other servals, including kittens.

The life

The serval needs access to water, and dense grassland. Reed beds, scrub or high grass are typical habitats. It is territorial.
